Sources

Original Spanish texts and digital editions

  • Don't Know No English
    Original: “Búcate plata” (“Go Get Money”) — Motivos de son (1930)
    Note: The poem's comic treatment of a man whose only English amounts to estrái guan (“strike one”) and guan tu tri is a hallmark of Motivos de son.
    Digital edition: Biblioteca Virtual Miguel de Cervantes — Sóngoro cosongo y Motivos de son
  • Wash Woman
    Original: “Lavandera” — Sóngoro cosongo: poemas mulatos (1931)
    Digital edition: Cervantes Virtual — Sóngoro cosongo
  • Cane
    Original: “Caña” — Sóngoro cosongo (1931)
    Full Spanish text available at Poemas del Alma and Poeticous
  • Blues
    Original: “Blues” — West Indies, Ltd. (1934)
    The opening line “Me matan si no trabajo, / y si trabajo me matan” directly corresponds to “I die if I don’t work / and if I do, I die.”
    Digital edition: Cervantes Virtual — West Indies, Ltd.
  • Sweat and Lash
    Original: “Sudor y látigo” — West Indies, Ltd. (1934)
    Digital edition: Cervantes Virtual — West Indies, Ltd.
  • Chop it With the Cane Knife!
    Original: “¡Hay que tener voluntad!” — West Indies, Ltd. (1934)
    Digital edition: Cervantes Virtual — West Indies, Ltd.
  • Two Kids
    Original: “Dos niños” — West Indies, Ltd. (1934)
    Digital edition: Cervantes Virtual — West Indies, Ltd.
  • That Kind of Soldier, Not Me
    Original: “No sé por qué piensas tú” — Cantos para soldados y sones para turistas (1937)
    Full Spanish text at Poeticous and Cervantes Virtual — Cantos para soldados
  • Little Song for the Children of the Antilles
    Original: “Un son para niños antillanos” — El son entero (1947)
    Note: Guillén recorded this poem himself; a recording is available through the Library of Congress
